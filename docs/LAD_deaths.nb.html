Bounds on covid imports

This paper estimate about 1400 imports to UK in the early pandemic, but states this is likely an underestimate. Peaks at around the end of our timeseries (13th March), with around half of those imports by then.

https://virological.org/t/preliminary-analysis-of-sars-cov-2-importation-establishment-of-uk-transmission-lineages/507

Estimates peak imports at around 60/day, so from, say first import on 13th Feb until 13th march, linear increase from nearly zero.

UKpop <- 67200000 # Google thinks this was the UK population in 2020

LADpop <- sum(deaths$popsize) # population of the LADs with deaths is around 10 million
popfrac <- LADpop / UKpop

# Assume half the importations (around 700) were by March 13th 

# If we assume those 700 went into all LADS, that implies only aboyt 1/7 (or 100) went into LADs with deaths
total_imports <- 700 * popfrac

Open questions

Pybus et al. acknowledge that their import rate is an underestimate. But by how much? 10%? 100%? 1000%?

How do we choose pini? Do we find some way to assimilate that? It seems to have a large impact on exposure.

It seems that we need to weight the imports to after mid February.
